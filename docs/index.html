Steps to buying a home.

Hire Your Agent. When you're looking for a real estate agent, know that good agents put their clients first. An agent should educate you about the current conditions of the market, analyze what you want and what you need in your next home, negotiate on your behalf to get you the best deal possible and suggest solutions to solve any problems that may arise.

Secure Financing. Ask your agent to introduce you to a preferred financing consultant. This professional will review your current financials and discuss the options available to you during the home purchasing process. You will be informed and updated of the entire financial process throughout your purchase.

Find Your Home. So you're ready to begin your search, but how or where do you start? Your agent will help you find homes that meet all your important needs, and as many of your additional wants as possible. Some questions you might ask yourself include: What amenities are crucial for you and your family? How much space do you need and why? How important is home value appreciation? What features are not negotiable in your new property?

Close. Once you've determined a price point you're happy with, don't put the property purchase at risk. You have a few responsibilities that you'll still need to keep in mind. It's important to keep your financials steady throughout the buying process. It's important to stay in constant communication with your agent and financial guide during this process. Also be sure to return all phone calls and complete paperwork promptly.

And remember, just because the sale is complete, your relationship with your agent doesn't need to end! After you buy, your agent can still provide information on the real estate market, find contractors and repair services, and even track your home's current value.
